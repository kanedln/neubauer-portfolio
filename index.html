Research Profile
Language, Learning & Computation

My work is grounded in applied linguistics and educational theory, with a sustained focus on language documentation, bilingual education, and instructional systems design. I have engaged in field-based research on Wisconsin German and contributed to language revitalization efforts, emphasizing culturally responsive methodologies.

In parallel, I design and implement computational pipelines to support linguistic inquiry. Utilizing tools such as Python, LangChain, and large language model frameworks, I build reproducible workflows for corpus analysis, semantic modeling, and automated data generation in research contexts.
